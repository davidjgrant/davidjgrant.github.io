Illustrated Book Covers

Role: Illustrator, Visual Designer, Printer
Sketches and illustrations, development on Adobe Illustrator, Typography choices and visual layout

The Penguin Random House tasked me with creating three book covers from their wide selection of classic books. The design for the three books had to carry the same theme and same typeface.

Having been a fan of the film, I knew before reading the book about how grand and theatrical F. Scott Fitzgerald's story would be. My design was inspired by the endless parties that Gatsby would host and the twisted relationship between him and Nancy. I drew out a house so big that it would be fitting to call a castle. By the the time I was developing it in Illustrator, I was in a repetitive mood of drawing many many windows. In the book, a green flashing light from is mentioned throughout the book but I wanted the colour of the cover to be purple. Purple has connotations of wealth and the story follows a world of very wealthy characters who seem to lack humanity. I imagined these parties lasting all night long with a purple-tint from fireworks, music and bottomless glasses of alcohol.

Told from the perspective of a curious child who is growing up in the American countryside, To Kill a Mockingbird is a staple of fine literature. There was a thrilling chapter in the book that I could not put the book down on and this is what inspired me to design the front cover. Two children playing which goes too far, one ends at the bottom of the road at the entrance of a mysterious family home, only to be seen through the eyes of children as a heart-pounding nightmare. Being told time and again to not get too close to that house, the children run away when they see a tall looming figure in the window watching them. This design captures a lot of action and mystery. The bright yellow contrasts well with the dark silhouettes as it represents a hot sunny day in the American countryside.

The Day of the Triffids was a thrilling read and was recommended to me by a friend. I enjoyed the story of the daily struggle to live in a post apocalyptic world with dangerous plants and how the power of sight was considered a gifted advantage. This book cover captures where the characters destination is by drawing a long road that ends in the city of London. I designed the book cover to make the reader ask more questions than answers. We do not know why the character wants to go there, or what those plants or doing scattering around or how it relates to comets in the sky. The green tone of the cover symbolises the great flash that blinds everyone and also represents the colour of the Triffids who are now humanities biggest threat.

The brief told me to do what every mum tells you not to do: Judge a book by its cover. But I had an amazing time diving into each story, and dragging out a small piece of information that could visually hint at the story but not give it away. The theme for each book was a dark silhouette of 2d layers that gave the design some perspective. The books also have a scratchy look to them to add texture and definition properties.
